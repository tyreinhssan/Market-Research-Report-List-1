<p><p>The plasma bench-top sterilizer market is segmented into low-temperature and high-temperature sterilizers. Low-temperature plasma sterilizers operate at conditions that are suitable for heat-sensitive materials, utilizing hydrogen peroxide plasma to ensure effective sterilization without damaging delicate instruments. In contrast, high-temperature sterilizers use elevated temperatures to achieve sterilization, often employing steam or dry heat, making them suitable for robust materials that can withstand higher thermal conditions. Both types cater to specific needs in healthcare and laboratory settings, ensuring safety and compliance.</p></p>
