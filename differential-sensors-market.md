<p><p>The differential sensors market includes two primary types: analog and digital sensors. Analog sensors provide continuous data representation, generating variable electrical signals that directly correlate with the measured quantity, making them ideal for applications requiring real-time monitoring. In contrast, digital sensors convert analog signals into discrete values, offering enhanced accuracy and ease of integration with digital systems. Both types cater to diverse industries, including consumer electronics, automotive, and industrial automation, driven by the growing demand for precision and automation in various applications.</p></p>
