<p><p>The photocurable resins market comprises three primary components: monomers, oligomers, and photoinitiators. Monomers are small molecules that polymerize upon exposure to light, forming the backbone of the resin. Oligomers are medium-sized compounds that enhance the properties of the resin, providing flexibility and strength. Photoinitiators are crucial for initiating the curing process, as they absorb light and generate free radicals, triggering polymerization. Together, these components enable the development of high-performance materials used in coatings, adhesives, and 3D printing applications.</p></p>
