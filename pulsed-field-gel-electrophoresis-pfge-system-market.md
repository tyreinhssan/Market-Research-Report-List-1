<p><ul><li>Rotating Gel Electrophoresis (RGE)</li><li>Field-Inversion Gel Electrophoresis (FIGE)</li><li>Transverse-Alternating Field Gel Electrophoresis (TAFE)</li><li>Contour-Clamped Homogeneous Electric Fields (CHEF)</li><li>Orthogonal-Field Alternation Gel Electrophoresis (OFAGE)</li><li>Programmable Autonomously-Controlled Electrodes (PACE)</li><li>Pulsed-Homogeneous Orthogonal Field Gel Electrophoresis (PHOGE)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Pulsed Field Gel Electrophoresis (PFGE) market includes various technologies for separating large DNA fragments. Rotating Gel Electrophoresis (RGE) enhances resolution through rotational movement. Field-Inversion Gel Electrophoresis (FIGE) alters the electric field direction for better separation. Transverse-Alternating Field Gel Electrophoresis (TAFE) uses alternating fields to enhance DNA migration. Contour-Clamped Homogeneous Electric Fields (CHEF) offers uniform fields for consistent separation, while Orthogonal-Field Alternation Gel Electrophoresis (OFAGE) employs orthogonal fields. Programmable Autonomously-Controlled Electrodes (PACE) automate processes, and Pulsed-Homogeneous Orthogonal Field Gel Electrophoresis (PHOGE) combines pulsed and orthogonal techniques for high-resolution separations.</p></p>
