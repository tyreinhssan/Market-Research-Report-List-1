<p><p>The ligament prostheses market consists of two main types: allograft prostheses and synthetic prostheses. Allograft prostheses utilize donated human tissue, providing a natural alternative for ligament reconstruction with good biomechanical properties and biocompatibility. In contrast, synthetic prostheses are made from artificial materials designed to mimic ligament function while offering durability and stability. Both types aim to improve patient outcomes in orthopedic procedures, with choices depending on factors such as the specific injury, patient preferences, and surgeon recommendations.</p></p>
